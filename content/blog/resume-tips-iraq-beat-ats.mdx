## What Is ATS and Why Are Iraqi Companies Using It?

An Applicant Tracking System is software that companies use to automatically collect, filter, and rank job applications. Think of it as a digital gatekeeper — it scans your resume for keywords, formatting, and relevance before deciding whether you're worth a human's time.

You might assume this is only a "Western" problem. Not anymore.

In Iraq and the Kurdistan Region, ATS adoption is growing fast — especially in:

- **International oil & gas companies** (TotalEnergies, Schlumberger, BP, Dana Gas)
- **INGOs and UN agencies** operating in Erbil and Sulaimani
- **Banks and financial institutions** (Kurdistan International Bank, Cihan Bank, etc.)
- **Telecom companies** (Korek, Asiacell, Zain)
- **Large local conglomerates** with HR departments

If you're applying through an online portal, LinkedIn, or a company website — there's a very good chance an ATS is screening you first.

## Top 7 Resume Tips Specific to Iraq's Job Market

### 1. Use Keywords from the Job Description — Literally

ATS systems match words. If the job says "project coordination" and your CV says "managing tasks," you might get filtered out even though you mean the same thing.

**What to do:** Copy the exact phrases from the job posting. If they say "stakeholder engagement," use that exact phrase. Don't paraphrase.

For jobs in **Erbil and Sulaimani**, also include location-specific terms where relevant — "Kurdistan Region of Iraq," "KRI," or city names when your experience is local.

### 2. Keep Formatting Simple and Clean

Fancy tables, text boxes, columns, and graphics look great to humans — but ATS systems often can't read them. Your carefully designed two-column CV might be parsed as a jumbled mess.

**What to do:**
- Use a single-column or simple two-column layout
- Avoid text boxes and images
- Stick to standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
- Use `.docx` or plain `.pdf` — not scanned images

### 3. Write a Strong Professional Summary With Keywords

The top of your resume is prime ATS real estate. A 3–4 line professional summary packed with relevant keywords can dramatically boost your ATS score.

**Example for a finance role in Erbil:**
> *"Certified accountant with 6 years of experience in financial reporting, IFRS compliance, and budget management within Kurdistan Region banking and corporate sectors. Proficient in SAP and QuickBooks."*

### 4. Quantify Your Achievements — Don't Just List Duties

ATS systems increasingly use AI to score resume quality. Bullet points that include numbers rank higher than vague statements.

**Weak:** *"Responsible for managing the sales team"*
**Strong:** *"Led a 12-person sales team, increasing quarterly revenue by 23% over 18 months"*

Numbers work across all sectors — NGO projects, oil field operations, IT deployments, or retail performance.

### 5. Use Standard Section Headings

Don't get creative with section names. ATS expects standard headers.

✅ Use: **Work Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications, Languages**
❌ Avoid: "My Journey," "What I've Done," "Career Highlights"

### 6. Include Both English and Arabic/Kurdish Versions

In Iraq, some ATS systems are set up by international companies that expect English-formatted names, while local systems might search in Arabic. List your name and key institutions clearly in English, and if applying locally, consider having a separate Kurdish or Arabic version.

### 7. Don't Ignore the Skills Section

This is one of the highest-weighted sections in ATS scoring. List both **hard skills** (AutoCAD, Python, SAP, Primavera P6, SPSS) and **soft skills** (leadership, communication, problem-solving).

For the Kurdistan job market, also list languages: **Kurdish (Sorani/Kurmanji), Arabic, English** — these are often filtered criteria.
